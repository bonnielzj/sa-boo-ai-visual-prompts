# Risk Checklist

Use this before turning research or AI outputs into client-facing work.

## Client privacy

- Do not upload raw client floor plans, contracts, names, addresses, family photos, budgets, or site photos to external AI/tools unless the user confirms permission.
- Redact: names, phone numbers, addresses, faces, children, artwork, security systems, exact budgets.
- Use generic descriptions for social posts unless the client has approved disclosure.

## Copyright and inspiration

- Use references for direction, not copying. Do not reproduce another designer's exact layout, artwork, logo, photo, or proprietary render.
- For brand work, check logo uniqueness and avoid over-reliance on generated marks.
- For AI outputs, verify commercial terms of the model/platform and keep source prompts/references documented.

## AI visual claims

- Label AI concept images as concept exploration; do not imply they are construction drawings or guaranteed final results.
- For interiors, verify: dimensions, code/safety, materials, joinery, waterproofing, ventilation, lighting, budget, lead time.
- For print/brand, verify: typography license, color mode, resolution, bleed, safe margin, legibility, trademark risk.

## Health / wellness / sustainability claims

- Avoid medical claims. Use “comfort, safety, accessibility, sensory experience” unless backed by specialist evidence.
- For low-VOC, formaldehyde-free, recycled, carbon, antibacterial, fireproof claims, cite product certificates or official documents.
- Avoid greenwashing language without proof.

## Social platform risk

- Avoid misleading before/after, fake client results, exaggerated budget claims, or “guaranteed viral” statements.
- If using client cases, get approval for images, location, budget, and timeline disclosure.
- Keep sharp Bonnie POV professional: critique the design logic, not the client/user.

## Research risk

- Note when a source is social/algorithmic, sponsored, paywalled, or based on a narrow sample.
- Cite dates for annual reports and product capabilities.
- When evidence conflicts, separate confirmed facts from interpretation.
