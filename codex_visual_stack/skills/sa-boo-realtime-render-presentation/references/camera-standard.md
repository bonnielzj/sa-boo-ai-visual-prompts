# Camera Standard

## Shot hierarchy

1. **Establishing view**: explains layout and atmosphere.
2. **Experience view**: eye-level user feeling from entry/sofa/dining/bed.
3. **Function view**: storage, kitchen, bathroom, work area, circulation.
4. **Detail view**: material junction, lighting, furniture, art, hardware.
5. **Before/after locked pair**: proves transformation value.

## Camera defaults

| Scene | Height | Focal length / FOV guidance |
|---|---:|---|
| Interior eye-level | 1400-1600mm | natural, avoid too wide |
| Seating view | 1000-1200mm | intimate, human perspective |
| Detail/material | 900-1400mm | closer, controlled depth |
| Small bathroom | 1400-1600mm | use wider angle carefully, correct verticals |
| Social cover | flexible | stronger composition and negative space |

## Composition rules

- Keep vertical lines vertical unless deliberately dynamic.
- Avoid ultra-wide distortion that makes rooms look fake.
- Use foreground/midground/background layers.
- Leave negative space for proposal annotations or social cover text.
- Use repeated view names across tools.

## Camera QA

- [ ] No walls/furniture unintentionally clipping the camera.
- [ ] Eye level feels human.
- [ ] Vertical correction on.
- [ ] Focal length not distorting design.
- [ ] Camera order tells a story.
- [ ] Before/after pairs are locked to same camera.
