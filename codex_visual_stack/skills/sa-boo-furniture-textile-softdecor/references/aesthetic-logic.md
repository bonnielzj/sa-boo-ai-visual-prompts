# SA&BOO Aesthetic Logic for Furniture, Textile, and Soft Decor

This file turns Bonnie/SA&BOO taste into explicit review logic. Use it when critiquing, choosing, or styling furniture and soft decoration.

## Core position

SA&BOO furniture taste should be **quietly memorable**: disciplined proportion, tactile depth, contemporary restraint, and one poetic detail. It should feel authored, not like a catalog pasted into a room.

```text
高级 ≠ expensive object
高级 = proportion + material truth + restraint + spatial fit + craft detail + emotional specificity
```

## The 12 aesthetic questions

1. What is the object’s silhouette from 5 meters away?
2. Does its visual weight match the room volume and floor/wall materials?
3. Does it improve the ritual of the space or only fill emptiness?
4. Is the texture scale visible enough to matter but not noisy?
5. Are edges, legs, seams, handles, and bases intentional?
6. Does the textile choice support light, touch, cleaning, and use?
7. Is there one contrast pair: hard/soft, matte/gloss, dense/airy, old/new, East/West?
8. Does the piece age well, physically and aesthetically?
9. Can it be drawn, modeled, and sourced without lying?
10. Does it avoid fake luxury signals: random gold, over-tufting, copied “hotel lobby” clichés?
11. Does it connect to architecture: axis, window, wall panel, rug, lighting, artwork?
12. If removed, would the room lose meaning? If not, it may be filler.

## SA&BOO style spectrum

Use these as adjustable dials, not labels:

- **Calm architectural**: low contrast, strong geometry, large surfaces, few objects.
- **East-West tactile luxury**: natural fibers, stone/wood/bronze/silk, ritual objects, controlled ornament.
- **Gallery domestic**: sculptural furniture, negative space, art-object hierarchy, precise lighting.
- **Soft modern feminine**: curves, bouclé/wool/linen, warm neutrals, gentle reflections, not sugary.
- **Future craft**: chrome/liquid metal accents, technical textile, precise silhouettes, warm human grounding.
- **Heritage revival**: antiques, dark wood, patterned rugs, pleats, but cleaned by modern spacing.

## Anti-generic rejection list

Reject or revise:

- matching sets bought as a “suite” with no hierarchy;
- tiny rugs, short curtains, wrong sofa scale;
- luxury clichés: gold strips everywhere, marble on every surface, meaningless fluting;
- AI render objects that cannot be sat on, opened, cleaned, or built;
- over-styled trays/books/vases before the main furniture scale is solved;
- fabric choices that photograph well but fail daily use;
- “Pinterest style” without client ritual or site logic.

## High-value visual signals

Train the eye to notice:

- low, wide silhouettes that calm a room;
- legs/base shadow gaps that lighten heavy forms;
- seam lines that reveal cushion construction;
- curtain stack-back and ceiling integration;
- rug edge and pile direction;
- mixed textile tactility within one palette;
- object asymmetry used deliberately;
- patina: leather, bronze, wood grain, stone veining;
- one focal art/light/object rather than many competing props.

## Writing style for client-facing furniture logic

Good language is specific and visual:

- Weak: “This sofa is very high-end.”
- Better: “低矮而宽的沙发体块压低视觉重心，让客厅更安静；亚麻混纺表面保留自然纤维颗粒，与石材茶几形成冷暖触感对比。”

- Weak: “Use some soft decoration to make it cozy.”
- Better: “先用大面积羊毛地毯和落地帘建立柔软边界，再用两个低饱和靠包微调色温，避免小摆件堆砌。”
