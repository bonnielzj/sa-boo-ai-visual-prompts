# Source Map: Furniture, Textile, Soft Decor, CAD/SU Assets

Use this map to choose sources. Prefer official pages, open APIs, museums, standards bodies, supplier product docs, and clearly licensed GitHub/open datasets. Always record capture date and rights.

## Source categories

Treat high-end official furniture websites as the **first-cycle learning target** because they combine product design, real dimensions, CAD/SKP/BIM/3D assets, high-resolution product and scene images, material/finish systems, catalogues, contract language, and current brand trend direction. ### 4. Other manufacturer/product sources

Use these after the P0 official-brand cycle or when a project needs office, contract, lighting, fabric, or rugs:

- Herman Miller / MillerKnoll product models and planning tools.
- Steelcase, Haworth, Knoll for office/contract planning data.
- Muuto / HAY for contemporary Scandinavian furniture and 2D/3D files where available.
- Flos / Artemide / Louis Poulsen for lighting families, photometry/downloads, scene imagery.
- Fabric houses: Kvadrat, Maharam, Gabriel, Camira, Dedar, Rubelli, Pierre Frey, Kravet, Sunbrella.
- Rug and curtain sources with technical PDFs and care data.

Do not scrape private downloads or bypass login. Save only metadata and links unless terms allow file reuse.

### 5. GitHub and open research datasets

Use for machine-readable furniture scenes, assembly logic, and model/scene research. Treat licenses carefully.

- Search terms: `furniture dataset`, `3D furniture models`, `interior scene dataset`, `IKEA assembly dataset`, `OpenRooms dataset`, `3D-FUTURE dataset`, `ShapeNet furniture`.
- Store repository URL, license, dataset access terms, file formats, and allowed use.
- Do not import research models into commercial client work unless the license explicitly permits it.

### 6. Forums and community knowledge

Use forums for practical experience, not as final authority:

- upholstery and textile care discussions;
- SketchUp component cleanup/modeling techniques;
- woodworking/joinery forums;
- interior design forums and professional discussions;
- Reddit/StackExchange-style threads for anecdotal failure modes.

Mark as `community-anecdote`; verify with official specs before specifying.

## Rights policy

- `public-domain` / `CC0`: can often be reused with attribution habits, but still record source.
- `open-license`: record exact license and obligations.
- `reference-only`: private study only; do not paste into public/client deliverables.
- `supplier-reference`: use for product selection/specification; follow brand terms.
- `user-provided`: keep confidential unless user says otherwise.
- `unknown`: do not reuse; only record metadata or ask for permission.
